⚡ Circuit Breaking — fail fast to stay up

Cap how much load a service will accept. Beyond the limit, callers get an instant 503 instead of piling onto an already-struggling service and dragging the whole system down.

💡
The motivation. When a service slows down, callers keep sending requests, which queue up, which makes it slower, which makes callers retry… a cascading failure that takes down healthy services too. A circuit breaker (named after the electrical kind) trips once load crosses a threshold: extra requests are rejected immediately so the struggling service can recover — and the caller isn’t left hanging.

1 What the demo did

It deployed httpbin (a simple target) and fortio (a load generator), then ran the same load test twice — with 3 callers hitting it in parallel:

RunCircuit breakerResult (typical)
BaselinenoneCode 200 : 100%
With breakermax 1 conn / 1 pending200 ≈ 45% · 503 ≈ 55% (tripped)

The Sockets used line jumps too — Envoy is opening/closing connections as it rejects the overflow. Push concurrency higher (-c 5, -c 10) and the 503 share grows.

2 The policy: a DestinationRule

kind: DestinationRule
spec:
  host: httpbin
  trafficPolicy:
    connectionPool:
      tcp:  {maxConnections: 1}                 # at most 1 TCP connection
      http: {http1MaxPendingRequests: 1,        # +1 waiting in line
             maxRequestsPerConnection: 1}
    outlierDetection:                            # eject unhealthy pods
      consecutive5xxErrors: 1
      interval: 1s
      baseEjectionTime: 3m
      maxEjectionPercent: 100

connectionPool = the limit

How many connections / queued requests a caller may have to this host. Cross it and Envoy returns 503 UO (“upstream overflow”) instantly — no waiting.

outlierDetection = eviction

Passive health checking: if a specific pod returns errors, Istio ejects it from the load balancing pool for baseEjectionTime, so traffic avoids the bad instance.

3 Verify & explore

  1. Trip it harder — more concurrency = more 503: 
    kubectl exec deploy/fortio -c fortio -- fortio load -c 10 -qps 0 -n 60 http://httpbin:8000/get
  2. See Envoy’s overflow counter climb: 
    kubectl exec deploy/fortio -c istio-proxy -- pilot-agent request GET stats | findstr upstream_cx_overflow
    (macOS/Linux: replace findstr with grep.)
  3. Remove the breaker and re-run step 1 — back to ~100% 200: 
    kubectl delete -f circuit-breaker.yaml

4 Why this matters

  • Stops cascading failure: the #1 cause of “the whole site went down because one service was slow.”
  • Back-pressure: a fast 503 lets callers fall back or retry elsewhere instead of blocking.
  • Automatic bad-pod eviction: outlier detection routes around a sick replica with no human in the loop.
  • Zero app code: the limits live in a DestinationRule, tunable per-service without redeploys.
